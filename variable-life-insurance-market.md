<p><p>Variable life insurance is a type of permanent life insurance that combines a death benefit with an investment component. In the fixed premium insurance market, policyholders pay a consistent premium, with the investment risks and rewards linked to sub-accounts, often including stocks or bonds. In contrast, variable universal life insurance offers flexibility, allowing policyholders to adjust premiums and death benefits, while also participating in investment performance. Both markets cater to individuals seeking financial protection alongside wealth growth opportunities.</p></p>
