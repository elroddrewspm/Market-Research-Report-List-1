<p><p>The sterilization monitoring market encompasses three main types: biological monitoring, chemical monitoring, and mechanical monitoring. Biological monitoring involves using living organisms, such as spores, to ensure that sterilization processes effectively eliminate microorganisms. Chemical monitoring employs indicators that change color or alter in response to specific sterilization parameters, providing visual confirmation. Mechanical monitoring focuses on the operational functionality of sterilization equipment, ensuring proper performance through parameters like temperature and pressure. Together, these methods ensure effective sterilization in healthcare and laboratory settings.</p></p>
