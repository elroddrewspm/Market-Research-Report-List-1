<p><p>SDS Poly Acrylamide Gel Electrophoresis (SDS-PAGE) is a widely used technique in molecular biology and biochemistry for separating proteins based on their size. The method utilizes a gel matrix made from polyacrylamide, which provides a medium for protein migration under the influence of an electric field. The gel contains sodium dodecyl sulfate (SDS), a detergent that denatures proteins and imparts a negative charge, ensuring that the separation is based primarily on molecular weight rather than shape or charge.</p><p>The SDS-PAGE market is experiencing significant growth, driven by the increasing demand for protein analysis in various fields such as biotechnology, pharmaceuticals, and diagnostics.
