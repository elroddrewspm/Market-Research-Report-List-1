<p><p>Continuous Emission Monitoring Systems (CEMS) are essential for tracking air pollutants emitted by industrial processes. The market for CEMS includes two primary types: Extractive CEMS and In-Situ CEMS. Extractive CEMS collect gas samples from the emission source, analyze them in a lab setting, and provide detailed data on composition. In contrast, In-Situ CEMS measure emissions directly within the emission source using sensors, offering real-time monitoring. Both systems ensure compliance with environmental regulations and facilitate better emissions management.</p></p>
