<p><p>The 3D camera market consists of two main types: target cameras and target-free cameras. Target cameras are designed to capture 3D images by focusing on specific subjects or objects, relying on markers or predefined elements for accuracy. In contrast, target-free cameras utilize advanced algorithms and depth perception to create 3D images without needing predefined markers, enabling greater flexibility and spontaneity in capturing scenes. Both types cater to varying applications, from professional filmmaking to augmented reality and gaming.</p></p>
