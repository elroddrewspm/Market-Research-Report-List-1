<p><p>Gallium Arsenide High Electron Mobility Transistors (HEMTs) are categorized into two primary market types: enhanced and depletion mode. Enhanced mode HEMTs are normally-off devices, meaning they require a voltage to conduct, which enhances safety and reduces power consumption in applications. In contrast, depletion mode HEMTs are normally-on devices that conduct in the absence of gate voltage, providing high-speed switching capabilities. Both types are critical in RF and microwave applications, as well as in power amplifiers for telecommunications and satellite systems.</p></p>
